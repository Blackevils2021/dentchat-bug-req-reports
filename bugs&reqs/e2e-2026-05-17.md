## 3. Bug found and fixed during this test

**BUG (fixed): manual-claim card was unreachable for the exact users it targets.**
`ClaimPage` hard-gated on missing `id_card_number` and showed only "complete your profile"; the manual-request card lived solely in the *has-id-card-but-no-candidates* branch. A runner with no/blocked ID card — precisely the sub-0.50 case the feature exists for — could never reach it.
**Fix applied:** the manual-request card now also renders in the no-ID-card branch (alongside the profile guidance). Re-tested → PASS (screenshot 01). This is in the local working tree and will ship when the frontend is next deployed.

## 5. Backend integrity observations (worth the team knowing)

- **Trigger-safety of the new `requested` status confirmed in practice.** A `requested` insert fires no auto-approval/notification; only the organizer's requested→confirmed/rejected transition triggers the runner notification (verified via `notifications` table). The migration's trigger-safety analysis held under real prod triggers.
- **`fn_merge_runners` is atomic and complete** — claims, conflict resolution, deactivation, and audit all happen in one transaction; the merged claim kept its `confirmed` status.
- **`fn_reassign_claim` validates correctly** — both guards (self-reassign; target-already-has-claim) raise and make zero changes.
- **DB-level guarantee:** a partial unique index (`idx_claims_one_confirmed_per_result`) enforces **at most one confirmed claim per result** — observed when seeding (a 2nd confirmed claim on a result was correctly rejected, 23505).
